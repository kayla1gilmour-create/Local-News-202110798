Evaluating the Eco Claims of Glasgow Commonwealth Games Venues

This year's Commonwealth Games in Glasgow will take place in only four venues to signify the city's focus on ecological impact.

For the second time, the Commonwealth Games will be held in Glasgow after the Australian State of Victoria pulled out due to costs. This year, Glasgow intends to focus on climate issues, selecting four venues situated within an eight-mile distance. The Glasgow Commonwealth Games website states that all venues will be within walking distance of a train station to minimise carbon emissions, as in line with the Glasgow Climate Pact.

Since the city hosted COP26 in 2021, it has gained a name amongst the leading green cities. It places fifth in the Global Destination Sustainability Index, and all of the Commonwealth venues have attained sustainable acknowledgement. Despite this, Glasgow's Climate Adaption Plan reveals that the city has a way to go if it wants to meet net zero goals.
